# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

0r1g1n is a technical project working on infrastructure that matters — verifying the authenticity of digital media in a world where synthetic content is increasingly indistinguishable from real. People contributing to this project come from journalism, cryptography, blockchain development, human rights work, and many other fields. That diversity of perspective is the project's strength.

We are committed to maintaining a community where everyone can contribute effectively, regardless of their background, experience level, or area of expertise. This means being direct about technical disagreements while being respectful of the people having them.

---

## Standards

**We expect contributors to:**

- Be honest and direct in technical discussions — say what you mean, provide evidence for claims, and change your position when the evidence warrants it
- Assume good faith — most misunderstandings are misunderstandings, not attacks
- Treat newcomers with patience — everyone was new once, and a question that seems basic often reveals a gap in the documentation
- Focus criticism on ideas, implementations, and arguments — not on the people who hold them
- Acknowledge when you are wrong or when you have learned something
- Keep discussions in public channels where the community can benefit from them

**We do not accept:**

- Personal attacks, insults, or harassment of any kind
- Discrimination based on race, ethnicity, nationality, gender, gender identity, sexual orientation, disability, age, religion, or any other characteristic
- Unwelcome sexual attention or contact
- Publishing private information about others without their consent
- Deliberate intimidation, threats, or sustained disruption of discussions
- Conduct that a reasonable person would find hostile or harmful

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in all project spaces — GitHub Issues, pull requests, Discussions, the project repository, and any other official community channel. It also applies when a contributor is representing the project in public settings.

---

## Enforcement

Instances of unacceptable behaviour may be reported to the project maintainers at 0r1g1nprotocol@proton.me.

All reports will be reviewed promptly and handled confidentially. The maintainers are committed to protecting the identity of reporters.

**Consequences for violations are proportional to severity:**

- **Warning** — for a first offence that is minor and addressed promptly
- **Temporary suspension** — for repeated minor offences or a single significant violation
- **Permanent removal** — for severe violations or sustained patterns of behaviour that harm the community

Decisions are made by the maintainers (or Steering Committee in Phase 2 governance) and are final unless new material information emerges.

## A Note on Technical Disagreement

Technical projects attract strong opinions. Disagreement about specification design, cryptographic choices, implementation approaches, and architectural trade-offs is not only normal — it is how good specifications get made. Vigorous, evidence-based technical debate is welcome and encouraged.

The line this Code of Conduct draws is not between strong opinions and mild ones. It is between disagreement about ideas and disrespect toward people. You may argue forcefully that a proposed interface is wrong. You may not argue that the person who proposed it is foolish, incompetent, or unwelcome.

When in doubt: say what you think, show your reasoning, and treat the person you're disagreeing with as someone who is also trying to get this right.
